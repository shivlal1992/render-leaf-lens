Grape Leaf Blight (Isariopsis Leaf Spot)

Pseudocercospora vitis

Fungus

In a Nutshell

  • Small, circular spots on leaves with a dark brown to black center.
  • Spots develop yellowish or reddish margins, often with a white, cottony growth on the underside of the leaf.
  • Leaves turn yellow, wilt, and die off. Reduced fruit production and defoliation.

Symptoms

Small, circular spots develop on the leaves, often with a dark brown to black center. The spots may have yellowish or reddish margins, and a white, cottony growth can be seen on the underside of the leaf. As the disease progresses, the leaves turn yellow, wilt, and die off, leading to defoliation and reduced fruit production.

Recommendations

Organic Control

Apply copper-based fungicides before dry weather. Foliar sprays of organic coating agents can also prevent the infection.


Chemical Control

Always consider an integrated approach with preventive measures together with biological treatments if available. Fungicide applications are important to control grape leaf blight, particularly in humid areas. Contact fungicides that coat leaves are effective before infections and do not trigger resistance in the fungi. Fungicides containing copper, chlorothalonil, or mancozeb can also be used as a preventive treatment.



What caused it?

Grape leaf blight is caused by the fungus Isariopsis clavispora. This fungus is a facultative parasite, meaning it can survive on dead plant material and infect living plants. It enters the plant through wounds or natural openings and spreads through wind, water, or insect vectors. The disease is more severe in periods of high humidity, warm temperatures, and prolonged wet conditions.

Preventive Measures

  • Use healthy grapevine material and resistant varieties.
  • Maintain good air ventilation and well-drained soil in the vineyard.
  • Monitor fields regularly and remove infected leaves and vines.
  • Use crop rotation and remove volunteer hosts in and around the vineyard.
  • Avoid excessive fertilization with nitrogen.
  • Use plant fortifiers and apply organic amendments to promote healthy plant growth.
  • Prune vines regularly to promote air circulation and reduce humidity.
  • Destroy infected plant material and debris after harvest.
