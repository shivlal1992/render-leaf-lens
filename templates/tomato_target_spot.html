Tomato Target Spot

Corynespora cassiicola

Fungus

In a Nutshell

  • Small, circular, dark brown to black spots on leaves
  • Spots may have a yellow halo or ring around them
  • Leaves may become distorted or curled
  • Fungal disease that thrives in warm, humid environments

Symptoms

Tomato target spot is a fungal disease caused by Corynespora cassiicola. The disease is characterized by small, circular, dark brown to black spots on the leaves. The spots may have a yellow halo or ring around them, and can be scattered or clustered on the leaf surface. As the disease progresses, the leaves may become distorted or curled, and the plant's growth may be stunted.

Symptoms can be seen on the foliage of tomatoes:

  • Small, circular, dark brown to black spots on leaves
  • Spots may have a yellow halo or ring around them
  • Leaves may become distorted or curled
  • Reduced plant growth and yield

Recommendations

Organic Control

  • Remove and dispose of infected leaves or plants to prevent the spread of the disease
  • Improve air circulation around plants to reduce humidity
  • Water plants at the base to avoid wetting the leaves
  • Use copper-based fungicides or bicarbonate-based products to control the disease
  • Introduce beneficial microorganisms, such as Trichoderma harzianum, to the soil


Chemical Control

  • Use fungicides specifically labeled for target spot control
  • Apply fungicides at the first sign of infestation and repeat as necessary
  • Rotate fungicides to avoid developing resistance


Select and apply ONLY ONE of the following products to your crops:

  • Chlorothalonil 75.0% WP
  • Copper oxychloride 50.0% WP
  • Mancozeb 80.0% WP


What caused it?

Tomato target spot is caused by the fungus Corynespora cassiicola, which thrives in warm, humid environments. The disease is often spread through contaminated soil, water, or infected plant material.

Preventive Measures

  • Use clean seeds and seedlings
  • Rotate crops to break the disease cycle
  • Remove and dispose of infected plants or debris
  • Improve air circulation around plants to reduce humidity
  • Avoid overwatering and ensure good drainage
  • Use resistant varieties if available locally
