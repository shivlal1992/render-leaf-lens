Squash Powdery mildew

Podosphaera xanthii

Fungus

In a Nutshell

  • White, powdery growth on leaves and stems
  • Premature defoliation and reduced fruiting
  • Distorted and stunted growth
  • Reduced fruit quality and yield

Symptoms

The first visible symptoms of Squash Powdery Mildew appear as white, powdery growth on the upper surface of leaves and stems. As the disease progresses, the growth becomes more dense and can cover the entire leaf surface, causing premature defoliation and reduced fruiting. Infected leaves may become distorted and stunted, and the overall growth of the plant can be affected. On fruits, the powdery growth can appear as a white, cottony coating, reducing fruit quality and yield.

Recommendations

Organic Control

  • Remove and destroy infected leaves and stems to prevent the spread of the disease
  • Improve air circulation by pruning plants to allow for more space between leaves
  • Water plants in the morning to allow for drying during the day, and avoid overhead irrigation
  • Apply a 5% solution of bicarbonate (baking soda) to infected areas to help control the disease
  • Consider using organic fungicides such as sulfur or copper-based products


Chemical Control

  • Always consider an integrated approach with preventive measures together with biological treatments if available
  • Protectant fungicides such as chlorothalonil or dinocap can be sprayed around planting to avoid the disease
  • Once powdery mildew has been detected, fungicides based on myclobutanil or difenoconazole can be used to control the development of the fungus
  • Ensure powdery mildew fungicides from different chemical groups are used to avoid the development of resistance


Select and apply ONLY ONE of the following products to your crops:

  • Triadimefon 25.0% WP
  • Myclobutanil 10.0% WP
  • Penconazole 10.0% EC
  • Sulphur 80.0% WDG
  • Dinocap 48.0% EC

Remember to always follow the product label instructions, take necessary safety precautions, and rotate fungicides to minimize the risk of resistance development.


What caused it?

Squash Powdery Mildew is a disease caused by the fungus Podosphaera xanthii. It survives the winter on infected leaves and stems, and begins to produce spores in the spring. These spores are dispersed by wind and can infect new leaves and stems, starting a new cycle of the disease. High humidity and temperatures between 60-80°F (15-27°C) favor the development of the disease.

Preventive Measures

  • Use tolerant or resistant varieties
  • Monitor orchards for signs of the disease.
  • Remove and destroy infected leaves and stems
  • Improve air circulation by pruning trees
  • Water trees in the morning and avoid overhead irrigation
  • Apply a 5% solution of bicarbonate (baking soda) to infected areas
  • Consider applying a fungicide in the dormant season to prevent the disease

Note: The above recommendations are general guidelines and may vary depending on the specific region, climate, and regulations. It is always best to consult with a local expert or authority for specific advice on managing Squash Powdery Mildew.
