ORANGE HAUNGLONGBING (CITRUS GREENING)

orange haunglongbing (citrus_greening)

Candidatus Liberibacter

Bacteria

In a Nutshell

  • Yellowing or mottling of leaves
  • Premature defoliation and reduced fruit production
  • Stunted growth and reduced tree vigor
  • Reduced fruit quality and yield

Symptoms

The first visible symptoms of Huanglongbing (Citrus Greening) appear as yellowing or mottling of leaves, often with an irregular or blotchy pattern. As the disease progresses, the yellowing becomes more pronounced, and the leaves may become distorted or stunted. Infected trees may exhibit premature defoliation, reduced fruit production, and stunted growth. The disease can also cause a reduction in fruit quality and yield.

Recommendations

Organic Control

  • Remove and destroy infected leaves and stems to prevent the spread of the disease
  • Use physical barriers, such as fine-mesh screens, to prevent the Asian citrus psyllid (ACP) vector from reaching the trees
  • Apply insecticidal soap or neem oil to control ACP populations
  • Consider using biological control agents, such as parasitic wasps, to control ACP populations
  • Maintain good tree health through proper nutrition, irrigation, and pruning practices


Chemical Control

  • Always consider an integrated approach with preventive measures together with biological treatments if available
  • Use insecticides, such as imidacloprid or dinotefuran, to control ACP populations
  • Apply antibiotics, such as oxytetracycline, to infected trees to help control the disease
  • Ensure that insecticides and antibiotics from different chemical groups are used to avoid the development of resistance


Select and apply ONLY ONE of the following products to your crops:

  • Imidacloprid 20.0% WP
  • Dinotefuran 20.0% SG
  • Oxytetracycline 25.0% WP
  • Thiamethoxam 25.0% WG

Remember to always follow the product label instructions, take necessary safety precautions, and rotate fungicides to minimize the risk of resistance development.


What caused it?

Huanglongbing (Citrus Greening) is a disease caused by the bacterium Candidatus Liberibacter asiaticus, which is transmitted by the Asian citrus psyllid (ACP). The bacterium survives in the phloem tissue of infected trees and can be transmitted to healthy trees through the feeding activity of ACP.

Preventive Measures

  • Use tolerant or resistant varieties
  • Monitor orchards for signs of the disease and ACP populations.
  • Remove and destroy infected leaves and stems
  • Use physical barriers to prevent ACP from reaching the trees
  • Apply insecticidal soap or neem oil to control ACP populations
  • Maintain good tree health through proper nutrition, irrigation, and pruning practices
  • Consider applying antibiotics in the dormant season to prevent the disease

Note: The control measures for Huanglongbing (Citrus Greening) are focused on controlling the Asian citrus psyllid (ACP) vector, as well as treating infected trees with antibiotics. It is essential to follow an integrated approach that combines preventive measures with biological and chemical controls to manage the disease effectively.
